Prediction Market Study

What you'll do

Imagine a jar holding 100 balls: 70 red and 30 blue. At the end of the session, one ball will be drawn at random. Your task is to decide, round by round, whether you think it'll be red or blue, and back your answer by buying shares.

A "Yes" share is a bet that red will be drawn. A "No" share is a bet that blue will be drawn. After the draw, every share on the winning side pays $1.00; shares on the losing side pay $0.

You start with $15.00 and play 25 rounds. Each round you can buy some Yes or No shares, or pass. Prices move as you trade. The whole thing takes about 15 minutes.

Before you start

A quick check before you start

Two short questions to make sure the payout rules make sense. You'll need both correct to continue — if you get one wrong, we'll show why and let you try again.

1. You bought 3 Yes shares earlier for $1.20. At the end of the session, a red ball is drawn. How much do you receive from those shares?

Not quite. Yes shares are a bet on red, and red was drawn — so each share pays $1.00. With 3 shares you'd receive $3.00. The price you paid ($1.20 in this example) doesn't change the payout. Try again.

2. You bought 5 No shares earlier for $2.00. At the end of the session, a red ball is drawn. How much do you receive from those shares?

Not quite. No shares win only if blue is drawn. Red was drawn, so the No shares expire worthless and you receive $0. Try again.
